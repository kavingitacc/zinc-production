Help & Documentation

Chemical Reaction

The zinc leaching process follows the reaction:

Zn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂

Molecular Weights

  • Zn = 65 g/mol
  • H₂SO₄ = 98 g/mol
  • ZnSO₄ = 161 g/mol
  • H₂ = 2 g/mol

Calculation Formulas

Parameter Formula
Pure Zn Zinc Ash × Zn% ÷ 100
Concentrated H₂SO₄ Pure Zn × 98 ÷ 65
Diluted Acid Concentrated H₂SO₄ × 2
Dilution Water Concentrated H₂SO₄
Process Water Total Process − (Zinc Ash + Diluted Acid)
Total Water Process Water + Dilution Water
ZnSO₄ Produced Pure Zn × 161 ÷ 65
Hydrogen Produced Pure Zn × 2 ÷ 65

Input Guide

  • Batch Number: Unique batch identification.
  • Engineer Name: Name of the responsible engineer (letters and spaces only).
  • Zinc Ash (kg): Weight of zinc ash used.
  • Zn % in Zinc Ash: Laboratory analyzed zinc purity.
  • Total Process (kg): Total desired batch size.
  • Reactor Capacity (L): Working reactor volume.
  • Reactor Utilization (%): Default 80%.

Output Explanation

  • Pure Zn: Amount of pure zinc available.
  • Concentrated H₂SO₄: Theoretical sulfuric acid required.
  • Diluted Acid: Concentrated acid after 1:1 dilution.
  • Dilution Water: Water required for acid dilution.
  • Process Water: Remaining water after deducting zinc ash and diluted acid.
  • ZnSO₄ Produced: Theoretical zinc sulfate produced.
  • Hydrogen Produced: Theoretical hydrogen gas evolved.

Safety Notes

  • Always add acid to water, never water to acid.
  • Wear appropriate PPE (Personal Protective Equipment).
  • Use corrosion-resistant equipment.
  • Ensure proper ventilation due to hydrogen evolution.
  • Follow plant SOPs during batch preparation.

Tips

  • Verify Zn% from laboratory analysis.
  • Ensure reactor capacity is sufficient before starting the batch.
  • Double-check inputs before calculating.
  • Print and attach the report to the batch file.
  • Use the printed report for production documentation.
